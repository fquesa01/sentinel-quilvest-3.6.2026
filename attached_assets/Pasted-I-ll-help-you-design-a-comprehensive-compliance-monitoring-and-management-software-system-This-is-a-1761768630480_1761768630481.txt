## **2. AI/ML Compliance Detection System**

### **Detection Mechanisms:**

**A. Rule-Based Detection (Deterministic):**

- Keyword/phrase matching (e.g., “bribe,” “kickback,” “off the books”)
- Regular expressions for sensitive data (account numbers, SSNs)
- Threshold triggers (unusual transaction amounts, frequency patterns)

**B. Machine Learning Models:**

- **NLP (Natural Language Processing):** Sentiment analysis, context understanding
- **Behavioral analysis:** Anomaly detection in communication patterns
- **Entity recognition:** Identifies people, companies, monetary amounts
- **Network analysis:** Maps relationships and communication clusters

**C. Regulatory Rule Sets:**

- **FCPA Module:** Anti-bribery, improper payments, third-party due diligence
- **Banking Regulations:** BSA/AML, OFAC sanctions, Reg E, Reg Z, GLBA
- **Antitrust:** Price-fixing language, market allocation discussions, bid-rigging
- **SEC Regulations:** Insider trading patterns, material non-public information
- **SOX Compliance:** Financial reporting accuracy, internal control violations
- **Florida-Specific:** Florida Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act, state securities laws

### **Violation Classification System:**

- **Critical (Red Alert):** Immediate legal exposure - requires instant escalation
- **High Priority (Orange):** Probable violation - requires investigation within 24 hours
- **Medium (Yellow):** Potential concern - review within 72 hours
- **Low (Blue):** Teaching moment - training recommendation
- **Informational (Green):** Flagged for awareness, no immediate action

## **4. Case Management System**

### **Workflow Automation:**

```
ALERT TRIGGERED → CASE CREATED → NOTIFICATION SENT → INTERVIEW SCHEDULED 
→ INTERVIEW CONDUCTED → TRANSCRIPT GENERATED → ATTORNEY REVIEW QUEUE 
→ DECISION (Close/Escalate/Remediate) → DOCUMENTATION → REPORTING
```

### **Case Record Components:**

- **Alert Details:** Violation type, severity, flagged communications
- **Employee/Vendor Profile:** Position, history, prior incidents
- **Timeline:** Complete chronology of related communications
- **Evidence Package:** All relevant emails, messages, documents
- **Interview Materials:** Scheduling, recordings, transcripts
- **Legal Analysis:** Attorney notes, conclusions, recommendations
- **Remediation Actions:** Training assigned, policy updates, disciplinary measures
- **Closure Documentation:** Final determination, lessons learned

### **Role-Based Access:**

- **System Administrator:** Full access, configuration
- **Compliance Officers:** Alert management, interview scheduling
- **Attorneys (Law Firm):** Case review, legal analysis, recommendations
- **Corporate Counsel:** Oversight, high-level review
- **Auditors:** Read-only access to closed cases
- **Employees (Limited):** Access to their own training/remediation materials

## **5. Interview Scheduling & Recording Platform**

### **Integration with Video Conferencing:**

- Google Meet, Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Webex
- Calendar integration (Google Calendar, Outlook)

### **Automated Workflow:**

1. **Alert triggers interview requirement**
1. **System checks calendars** for employee and interviewer availability
1. **Sends meeting invitations** with compliance documentation attached
1. **Creates video conference link** with recording enabled
1. **Pre-meeting briefing materials** sent to interviewers

### **Recording & Transcription:**

- **Live recording:** Audio and video captured to secure cloud storage
- **Real-time transcription:** AI-generated transcript with speaker identification
- **Post-processing:** Transcript cleaning, time-stamping, keyword indexing
- **Privilege protection:** Attorney-conducted interviews marked as privileged
- **Retention compliance:** Automatic retention schedules per policy

### **Interview Types:**

- **Fact-finding:** Initial inquiry into flagged communication
- **Investigative:** Formal investigation interview
- **Training:** Educational discussion (lower-severity flags)
- **Exit Interview:** When violations lead to termination

## **Critical Compliance & Legal Considerations**

### **A. Privacy & Employment Law:**

- **Employee consent:** Banner notifications, acceptable use policies
- **State wiretap laws:** Florida is single-party consent, but monitor employees in two-party states
- **ECPA compliance:** Stored Communications Act requirements
- **Union considerations:** Collective bargaining agreement implications
- **Reasonable expectation of privacy:** Corporate device vs. BYOD policies

### **B. Data Protection:**

- **GDPR implications:** If monitoring EU employees or communications
- **CCPA/Florida privacy laws:** Data subject rights (access, deletion requests)
- **Data minimization:** Only collect what’s necessary for compliance
- **Retention limits:** Automatic purging after regulatory periods expire

### **C. Attorney-Client Privilege:**

- **Privilege filters:** Flag communications with legal counsel
- **Ethics walls:** Segregate law firm communications from client data
- **Disclosure risks:** Careful design to avoid waiving privilege

### **D. Regulatory Approval & Cooperation:**

- **SEC/DOJ cooperation credit:** System demonstrates proactive compliance program
- **Federal Sentencing Guidelines:** Effective compliance program reduces penalties
- **Consent orders:** May satisfy monitoring requirements in settlements
