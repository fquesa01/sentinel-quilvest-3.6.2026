# APPENDIX: OBJECTION HANDLING

## "We already have Relativity"
**Response:** "Relativity is great if you have the budget and the staff. What do you do for matters that don't justify spinning up Relativity? The $50K case that still needs document review? That's where we fit. And our privileged AI and ambient intelligence features aren't available on Relativity at any price."

## "Our data is too sensitive"
**Response:** "That's exactly why we deploy to YOUR Azure environment. Your data never leaves your control. We can't see it. Most competitors host your data on their servers — we're the opposite."

## "We don't have Azure"
**Response:** "We can help you set it up, or we can deploy to a dedicated environment we manage for you with the same security guarantees. Most clients find their IT teams can spin up an Azure tenant in a few days."

## "How is AI privileged?"
**Response:** "Sentinel Counsel is structured as a law firm. When you use our privileged research feature, you're communicating with counsel. That's the legal relationship. Combined with customer-deployed architecture where data doesn't leave your environment, privilege arguments are as strong as we can make them."

## "What about hallucinations?"
**Response:** "That's why we use the two-stage methodology. We don't throw AI at your entire document universe and hope for the best. Boolean search first creates a relevant, manageable subset. Then AI works on focused data where it's reliable. For litigation and M&A work, accuracy isn't optional."

## "This seems like a lot of features"
**Response:** "You don't have to use everything. Start with the workflow that's hurting you most — usually document review or deposition prep. The platform grows with you."

## "Why haven't I heard of you?"
**Response:** "We're purpose-built for firms and companies that the big vendors ignore — the 95% of law firms with fewer than 10 attorneys, PE shops without massive analyst teams, GCs who can't justify enterprise software costs. That's not a market the incumbents are chasing."
