


A HUD 1 is used for real estate transactions.  Is a HUD 1 only for residential deals?  are there other closing statements for commercial?  if so, how many different types?

A HUD-1 Settlement Statement historically was used in most real estate closings involving federally related mortgage loans, but it is not limited to residential property. The distinction today is less about residential vs. commercial and more about whether the transaction is governed by RESPA / TRID rules.

Below is the clean framework used in practice.

1. HUD-1 Settlement Statement
Primary historical use:
Closing statement required under the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA).

Used for
Federally related mortgage loans

Historically most residential purchases

Also sometimes commercial loans when lenders used RESPA formats

Today
After the 2015 TRID rule, HUD-1 is mostly eliminated for typical residential purchases.

It is still used for:

Reverse mortgages

Home equity lines of credit (HELOCs)

Manufactured home loans not secured by land

Certain commercial or non-TRID loans where parties still prefer the format

2. Closing Disclosure (CD)
This replaced the HUD-1 for most consumer residential closings.

Used for
Residential mortgage loans covered by TRID

Specifically:

1–4 unit residential property

Consumer borrower

Lender financing

Purchase or refinance

Not used for
Commercial loans

Cash deals

Investment property loans made to business entities

Reverse mortgages

HELOCs

3. ALTA Settlement Statement
This is the most common closing statement for commercial transactions.

Developed by the American Land Title Association (ALTA).

Used for
Commercial real estate

Cash transactions

Entity borrowers

Investor deals

1031 exchanges

Versions
There are typically three versions:

ALTA Buyer Settlement Statement

ALTA Seller Settlement Statement

ALTA Combined Settlement Statement

Commercial closings almost always use these.

4. Custom Settlement Statements
Commercial transactions frequently use custom closing statements prepared by:

Title company

Closing attorney

Escrow agent

Institutional lender

These are often simply called:

“Settlement Statement”
or
“Closing Statement.”

There is no mandated federal format for commercial closings.

5. Loan-Specific Lender Settlement Statements
Large institutional lenders often require their own formats, especially for:

CMBS loans

Construction loans

Syndicated loans

Mezzanine financing

Examples include:

Lender Funding Statement

Sources and Uses Statement

Loan Disbursement Statement

These often accompany the main settlement statement.

Practical Summary
Document	Typical Use	Property Type
HUD-1	Legacy RESPA closings	Some residential, limited modern use
Closing Disclosure (CD)	TRID residential loans	Consumer residential
ALTA Settlement Statement	Standard commercial closing	Commercial
Custom Settlement Statement	Attorney/title prepared	Commercial or cash
Sources & Uses Statement	Financing summary	Commercial loans
How Commercial Closings Usually Work
Most commercial deals include three financial closing documents:

ALTA Settlement Statement

Sources and Uses Statement

Lender Funding Statement

To design a complete closing-statement system for a real-estate closing platform, you should think in terms of (1) regulatory documents, (2) settlement/closing statements, and (3) lender funding / disbursement schedules.

Across U.S. real estate transactions there are roughly 12–16 core financial closing statement formats that cover essentially every residential and commercial closing scenario. Below is a comprehensive taxonomy from simplest to most complex.

1. Consumer Residential Closings (TRID Regulated)
These are governed by RESPA + TILA + TRID rules and have mandatory federal forms.

1.1 Closing Disclosure (CD)
Standard form required for most residential mortgages.

Used for:

1–4 unit residential property

Consumer borrowers

Purchase or refinance

Mortgage financing

Typical structure:

Loan terms

Projected payments

Closing costs

Cash to close

Loan calculations

Entities involved:

Lender

Settlement agent

Buyer

1.2 Seller Closing Disclosure
Mirror version of the CD provided to the seller.

Some jurisdictions issue:

Combined CD

Separate seller CD

1.3 Loan Estimate (Pre-Closing but Required)
Not technically a closing statement but required in TRID workflows.

Your platform should support it because numbers must reconcile with the CD.

2. Residential Transactions Not Covered by TRID
Certain loans still use the HUD-1.

2.1 HUD-1 Settlement Statement
Legacy RESPA form.

Still used for:

Reverse mortgages

HELOCs

Manufactured home loans (no land)

Certain private loans

Key sections:

100 series: borrower charges

200 series: seller credits

700 series: broker commissions

800–1300: loan costs and escrows

2.2 HUD-1A
Simplified version used for refinances without seller involvement.

3. Cash Residential Closings
When no institutional lender is involved, title companies typically use:

3.1 Cash Settlement Statement
Often called:

Cash Closing Statement

Owner Settlement Statement

Prepared by:

Title company

Closing attorney

No mandated format.

4. Standard Commercial Real Estate Closings
Commercial deals usually use ALTA settlement formats.

4.1 ALTA Settlement Statement – Combined
Single statement showing buyer and seller.

4.2 ALTA Buyer Settlement Statement
4.3 ALTA Seller Settlement Statement
These are the most common commercial closing statements in the U.S.

Fields typically include:

Purchase price

Prorations

Loan payoff

Broker commissions

Transfer taxes

Escrows

5. Institutional Commercial Loan Closings
Large commercial loans include lender funding schedules.

5.1 Sources and Uses Statement
Shows:

Sources:

Loan proceeds

Equity

Deposits

Uses:

Purchase price

Fees

Escrows

Payoffs

Used in:

Commercial purchases

Development

recapitalizations

5.2 Lender Funding Statement
Also called:

Funding Authorization

Disbursement Authorization

Shows exactly how loan proceeds are disbursed.

5.3 Closing Funds Flow Statement
Often used by large law firms.

Shows precise wire movements.

Example:

Source	Amount	Destination
Bank loan	$20M	Escrow
Equity	$10M	Escrow
Escrow	$28M	Seller
Escrow	$2M	Reserves
6. Construction Loan Closings
More complex deals often include multiple funding schedules.

6.1 Construction Sources and Uses
Includes:

Hard costs

Soft costs

Interest reserves

Developer fees

6.2 Construction Draw Schedule
Tracks future loan disbursements.

6.3 Development Budget
Often attached to closing statements.

7. CMBS / Structured Finance Closings
Very large deals (office towers, hotels, malls).

Typical documents:

7.1 CMBS Funding Memo
Prepared by closing counsel.

Includes:

tranche funding

defeasance calculations

loan allocations

7.2 Structured Funds Flow
Shows:

mortgage

mezzanine

preferred equity

8. Multi-Layer Capital Stack Closings
Used in:

private equity real estate

opportunity zone deals

REIT transactions

Documents include:

8.1 Capital Stack Summary
Sources:

Senior debt

Mezzanine debt

Preferred equity

Sponsor equity

8.2 Investor Distribution Waterfall Statement
Used when equity investors close simultaneously.

9. 1031 Exchange Closings
Exchange accommodators require specific statements.

9.1 1031 Exchange Settlement Statement
Includes:

exchange funds

replacement property calculations

intermediary balances

9.2 Qualified Intermediary Funds Statement
Prepared by exchange company.

10. Portfolio and Bulk Asset Closings
Used when multiple properties close simultaneously.

10.1 Portfolio Settlement Statement
Shows:

allocation by property

prorations

multi-property taxes

10.2 Property Allocation Schedule
Important for accounting.

11. Leasehold / Ground Lease Transactions
Complex deals include:

11.1 Ground Lease Closing Statement
Includes:

prepaid rent

leasehold financing

leasehold taxes

12. REIT / Institutional Transactions
Very large deals use:

12.1 Master Closing Statement
Shows all transaction components.

12.2 REIT Contribution Statement
Used when properties are contributed into a REIT.
