### 🧭 **3. HR Investigation Procedure Checklist**

| Step | Description                | Legal Notes                                                                      |
| ---- | -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | **Receive Complaint**      | Document date/time/source; ensure confidentiality.                               |
| 2    | **Assess Urgency**         | If physical threat or safety issue → take immediate protective action.           |
| 3    | **Acknowledge Complaint**  | Send written confirmation to complainant; outline next steps.                    |
| 4    | **Select Investigator**    | Must be impartial – often HR or outside counsel.                                 |
| 5    | **Define Scope & Issues**  | Identify statutes/policies implicated (use section 2 table).                     |
| 6    | **Gather Documents**       | Emails, timecards, pay records, video, policy manuals.                           |
| 7    | **Interview Witnesses**    | Complainant → Accused → Witnesses; record statements.                            |
| 8    | **Evaluate Evidence**      | Apply “preponderance of evidence” standard.                                      |
| 9    | **Draft Findings Report**  | Include facts, credibility, policy violations, legal analysis.                   |
| 10   | **Take Corrective Action** | Discipline, retraining, termination, or remedial steps.                          |
| 11   | **Communicate Outcome**    | Inform complainant/accused within confidentiality limits.                        |
| 12   | **Maintain Records**       | Retain investigation file ≥ 1 year (Title VII); Florida best practice = 4 years. |
| 13   | **Follow-Up**              | Monitor for retaliation, recurrence, or policy improvements.                     |
